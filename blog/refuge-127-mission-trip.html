OUR 2017 REFUGE 127 MISSION TRIP

By Vera Holloway  ·  2017

Earlier this year I had the incredible privilege of traveling to Uganda, Africa with Refuge 127 — an organization that is very near and dear to my heart. I currently serve as the Board Vice President, and this trip was a powerful reminder of why this work matters so much.

How It Started

The story of Refuge 127 begins with my sister-in-law, Mandy. Together, we felt called to respond to the need for orphan care in Uganda, and what started as a small act of obedience has grown into something that continues to change lives on both sides of the world. Watching God move through this organization has been one of the greatest joys of my life.

The Journey to Kampala

After a long journey, we arrived in Kampala — the capital city of Uganda — and immediately felt the warmth of the people there. From Kampala, we made our way out to the villages where Refuge 127 does its most hands-on work. The roads are rough, the scenery breathtaking, and the anticipation of what we were about to witness was something I cannot fully put into words.

Kenkebu and Kachoma Villages

Our visits took us to orphanage sites in two villages: Kenkebu and Kachoma. These communities are home to children who have lost parents — many to illness, poverty, or circumstances beyond anyone's control. What struck me most was not the need (though it is great) but the joy. The children ran to greet us. They sang. They held our hands. They have so little by the world's standards and yet radiate something most of us spend our whole lives searching for.

We were blessed to work alongside Bishop John Wayabire, a man of deep faith and immense dedication to these children. His leadership on the ground is irreplaceable, and his love for each child is evident in everything he does.

What Has Been Built

Through the generosity of donors and the hard work of volunteers, Refuge 127 has accomplished remarkable things in these villages:

  • 4 classrooms — giving children access to education they would not otherwise have
  • A girls' dormitory — providing safe shelter for orphaned girls
  • Water wells — access to clean water, which changes everything for health and daily life
  • Restrooms — basic sanitation that most of us take completely for granted

Each one of these things was built through sacrifice and faith — and through people who said yes when God asked them to show up.

What Still Needs to Be Done

The work is far from finished. There is still so much need, and our hearts are set on continuing to meet it. Here is what is still on the horizon for Refuge 127:

  • A boys' dormitory — the girls have safe shelter; the boys need the same
  • A multi-purpose building — a space for community gatherings, church, training, and more
  • Farming development — sustainable agriculture to help the community feed itself long-term

Will You Help?

If this story stirred something in you, I want to invite you to learn more and consider getting involved. Every dollar given, every prayer offered, and every person who shares the story of Refuge 127 makes a real difference in the lives of children who simply need someone to show up for them.

Visit www.refuge127.org to learn more, donate, or find out how you can be part of this mission.

To the children of Uganda — you changed me more than I could ever change you. I'll be back.
