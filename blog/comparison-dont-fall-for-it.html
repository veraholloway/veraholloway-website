Comparison – don’t fall for it!

By Vera Holloway

We all fall victim to comparison. Every single one of us. Even the counselor who is sitting down to write this article — I almost didn't write it after finding out that my friend Alli Worthington had already written something on this very topic. The irony was not lost on me. There I was, comparing my article before it even existed.

And yet — here we are. Because I think there is still something worth saying, and maybe I'm the right person to say it to you today.

Even the Disciples Did It

Comparison is not a new problem. It is as old as humanity itself. In Matthew 18:1–4, the disciples came to Jesus with a question that reveals just how deeply this tendency runs:

"At that time the disciples came to Jesus and asked, 'Who, then, is the greatest in the kingdom of heaven?' He called a little child to him, and placed the child among them. And he said: 'Truly I tell you, unless you change and become like little children, you will never enter the kingdom of heaven. Therefore, whoever takes the lowly position of this child is the greatest in the kingdom of heaven.'" — Matthew 18:1–4

Even the men who walked with Jesus — who witnessed miracles firsthand — were asking who was greatest. Who was doing better. Who ranked higher. Jesus' answer? Humility crushes comparison. The path up is down. The way to greatness is to become like a child — dependent, trusting, and entirely unconcerned with status.

The Counselor's Plan

So how do we actually do this? Here is a practical, four-part plan I share with my clients — and remind myself of regularly.

1

Flip the Script

The battle against comparison begins in the mind. Dr. Caroline Leaf's research shows that we can literally change the cellular structure of our brains by changing our thoughts. What we repeatedly think shapes who we become. So when comparison creeps in, we flip the script: Who does God say I am? Find your identity in Christ, not in what you see on someone else's highlight reel. A go-to verse for this: Psalm 27:1 — "The Lord is my light and my salvation — whom shall I fear?"

2

Avoid the Ultimate Distraction

Social media is the single greatest engine of comparison in our culture. It is designed to make you measure. If you find yourself spiraling, set hard limits on your usage — or consider a full fast. This is not weakness. This is wisdom. You cannot win a war if you keep walking back into the battlefield unarmed.

3

Attitude of Gratitude

Gratitude is the single best antidote to comparison I have ever found — in the research, in the counseling room, and in my own life. It is physically impossible to be fully grateful and fully envious at the same time. Start a gratitude practice today. Not just a mental note — write it down. Three things, every morning. Watch what happens over time.

4

Show Up to the Table

Bring what you have and watch what God does. The disciples had five loaves and two fish — it wasn't much by anyone's measure, but placed in the right hands it fed thousands. You don't need to be the most talented, the most polished, or the most experienced. You just need to show up with what you've got. Your story. Your voice. Your gifts. Stop waiting until you're as good as someone else. Someone needs exactly what you have right now.

Comparison will keep coming for you — that is the truth. But every time it does, you now have a plan. Flip the script. Guard your eyes. Choose gratitude. Show up anyway.

You were made for this. Don't let comparison tell you otherwise.
